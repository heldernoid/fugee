Your Rights — Summary Card

Key protections for people seeking asylum · Prepared with Fugee

Non-refoulement

You cannot be forced back to a country where your life or freedom would be at serious risk. This is a core protection under the 1951 Refugee Convention.

Your rights while your claim is decided

  • The right to seek asylum and have your claim heard fairly.
  • The right to an interpreter and to free legal assistance.
  • The right not to be detained arbitrarily.
  • The right to basic dignity: shelter, food, and emergency healthcare.
